Brother Aldric does not move quickly. He sits where he is allowed to sit and speaks with whoever will listen. Dorogh's guards have tried to move him on. He goes when they insist, finds another corner, sits down again.

He is not afraid of Dorogh in any observable way. This disturbs the guards more than defiance would. You cannot intimidate someone who has already made peace with the worst that can happen.

He keeps the old prayers alive by saying them quietly, to anyone who will hear, and some who won't.

## GM Notes

Aldric is not a source of tactical information. He is a source of grounding and perspective — useful when the party is frightened or discouraged.

He speaks in long sentences that feel like they're going somewhere slowly. They always arrive.

**Voice:** "I have watched this city hold its breath for a long time. Breath held long enough becomes a kind of prayer. Something is about to exhale."

**Role in prep:** Use Aldric when you want to give the players a moment of stillness before something difficult, or when they need someone to remind them of what they're fighting for beyond the immediate obstacle.
