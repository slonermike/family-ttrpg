> [!DIALOG]
> **Murn:** "You're not Goob."

> [!NARRATION]
> His ears swivel toward you — enormous, dish-like, rotating independently. His face is smooth where eyes would be.

> [!DIALOG]
> **Murn:** "Goob went in. That was... I don't know how long ago. Will you go in?"

Murn is a Wumble — small, round, velvet-furred, with no eyes and ears like satellite dishes. He navigates entirely by sound, smell, and touch. He has been sitting at the ridge top since Goob went into the cavern and didn't come back.

He tried to follow. The resonance inside the cave — something about Blind Anger's presence — produces a sound his ears can't process. Disorienting to the point of helplessness. He sat back down outside and waited.

He's not panicking. He's just sitting. He has very good patience.

## In the Boss Battle

Murn comes back. He followed the party in after all — just stayed near the entrance until he heard Goob's voice again. When the party reaches the inner chamber, Murn and :npc[goob] are there at the edge of the dark.

They can't fight. But Blind Anger can't hide from them. In total darkness, they track his every movement by breath and footfall — and they tell the party.

**Each player gets one free re-roll** (attack or defense) during the Blind Anger encounter. When a player uses theirs, describe what Murn whispered just before the moment.

## GM Notes

Don't play him as helpless or pitiful. He's scared, but he's composed. He made a decision (wait) and he's sticking to it. His ears move constantly — he's reading the world, even if you can't see it.

He doesn't ask for promises. If the party says they'll try, he accepts that. He just needed to ask someone.

**Heart 8+ (optional):** A player who actually listens — not just "we'll help" but engages with him — earns this: *"There's something large in the deep part. It moves like it's angry. It doesn't move like it can see."*
