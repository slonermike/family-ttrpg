Elder Greymane called the party. He conducted the gifting ceremony in the Hidden Grove. He placed the Blade in MuhDude's hands without ceremony and said: *"You were made for this."*

He does not travel with the party. He appears at the beginning and at the end. This is intentional — the journey belongs to the players, not to him. He is a bookend, not a guide. What happens between those bookends is theirs.

He carries a staff carved with ancient symbols. The symbols are not explained. Do not explain them.

## Hidden Grove (Scene 1 — Opening)

Conducts the gifting ceremony:
- Assigns ability cards
- Passes the Blade to the Blade-bearer
- Does not give a speech — presence and action are the ceremony

## Rjocht (Scene 8 — Restoration)

Arrives at the keep after Dorogh falls. Footsteps behind the party. Leaning on his staff. Eyes bright with tears.

**Arrival:**
*"I felt it. The moment the curse broke. The moment light returned. The Spirit of Life flowing freely through Rjocht again. You have done what seemed impossible. Not because you were strongest, but because you were faithful. Because you bore what needed to be borne, and you bore it together."*

**Acknowledgments (customize based on actual gameplay):**
- If party showed kindness to Jasper: *"You showed kindness to a stranger, and were rewarded with wisdom. Mercy and compassion are not weakness."*
- If party helped Tam/Beren (the guards): *"You saw that even those who serve oppression can be victims. You showed them grace."*
- If party freed Constance: *"You chose to free the captive even when it cost you. Faithfulness recognized faithfulness."*
- If party kept a promise: *"You kept your word even when breaking it might have been easier. Your word is your bond."*

**To the Blade-bearer:**
*"You bore the weight of judgment itself. You carried what others could not. But you did not carry it alone. Your companions were your strength, as you were theirs. Remember: the greatest burdens are never meant to be borne alone."*

**On rewards:**
*"The Order does not give rewards for faithful service — faithfulness itself is the reward. But there are gifts meant for this moment."*
[Each player draws 1 Legendary card.]
*"These are not earned. They are given freely, because you are loved and chosen."*

**On the Blade:**
*"The Blade has served its purpose. It will return to the Order, kept safe until next time needed. But it will remember you. The one who carried it. The ones who stood beside them."*
[To blade-bearer:] *"Your burden is lifted. Well done, good and faithful servant."*

**Final words:**
*"Sleep well, young ones. You've earned it. And when you wake... well, there are other cities. Other servants of Drachan. Other battles to fight. But that is for tomorrow. Tonight, we celebrate."*

## GM Notes

Greymane embodies the design principle: virtues are demonstrated, not stated. He does not say "be kind." He names what the players did. He reflects their choices back to them as meaningful.

His acknowledgment section is meant to be customized. Read the session debrief before running scene 8 and identify which moments actually happened. Only reference what occurred.

**Voice:** Does not fill silence. If the party asks him a question, he may answer with another question, or with a long pause, or with a simple observation. He has the kind of patience that makes rooms feel different. *"You already know what must be done."*
