Margrave runs the most prominent merchant operation in Rjocht's market. He has survived Dorogh's rule by being indispensable — he manages goods, moves product, greases the wheels that keep commerce functioning. He tells himself this makes him pragmatic. It does. It also makes him complicit, which he has chosen not to examine.

He is not dangerous. He is proud, loud, and occasionally useful, which is its own kind of hazard.

## Session 1 Connection

MuhDude challenged Margrave to lift the Blade of Righteous Judgment. Margrave, being Margrave, could not resist. He tried. He could not move it. This is probably how the party acquired the Merchant's ID — either as a bet won, a trade made in the chaos, or a document dropped in the commotion.

## GM Notes

**Voice:** Speaks about himself in superlatives. Has an opinion on everything. Does not notice when people are not impressed. *"That? That's a SWORD. I've seen swords. That is not a normal sword. I'll give you twelve silver for it."*

**Use when:** The party needs a comic-relief encounter, a market connection, or someone whose pride can be played. He can be made to help if his ego is properly stroked. He responds to flattery like a plant to sunlight.

**Post-liberation:** Margrave will absolutely claim he knew the heroes the whole time and practically organized the uprising himself.
