Merra keeps a market stall and sells what Dorogh's rules allow — which is less every season. Before, her stall smelled of dried lavender and mint and a dozen things she could name in four languages. Now it smells of nothing permitted.

She still shows up. She still greets people with the same warmth. The small kindnesses she can offer — a kind word, a fair price, knowing someone's name — she gives freely.

## GM Notes

Merra is not a source of information so much as a source of humanity. She reminds the players that this city is full of people, not just oppression.

She may know herbal remedies, have small useful items tucked away, or be able to point the party toward others who can help. She is not brave in the conventional sense — she is quietly, daily brave in the way that ordinary faithfulness is brave.

**Voice:** Knows everyone's name. Remembers what they usually buy. Offers things even when she has little. "I saved these for someone who needed them."

**If given a kind moment by the party:** her eyes go bright, briefly. She turns away before they see her wipe them.
