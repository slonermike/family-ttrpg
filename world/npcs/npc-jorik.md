Jorik runs a smithy near the inner wall in Rjocht's eastern district. He stayed when others left or were driven out. He is still here, still making things, still giving what he can to people who need it.

He is not impressive to look at. He is enormous, soot-covered, and chooses his words like he's spending something precious.

## Talking Points

### At the Forge — Rjocht

:::details[🌫️ The Fog | Jorik laments the fog; remembers the sun shining on Rjocht]
> [!DIALOG]
> **Jorik:** "The fog's been here longer than I can remember."

> [!NARRATION]
> He looks toward the wall. There's no window. He looks anyway.

> [!DIALOG]
> **Jorik:** "I used to watch the sun come up over the eastern wall. Turn everything gold." A pause. "My daughter used to climb up to see it."

> [!NARRATION]
> He goes quiet. Picks up the hammer. Doesn't swing it yet.

> [!DIALOG]
> **Jorik:** "Fog doesn't last forever. Nothing does." He looks at you. "You hold onto that."
:::

:::details[🕯️ His Daughter | The forge brings her out of him; he prays for her abundance, not just her safety]
> [!NARRATION]
> He doesn't start with her. The forge brings it out of him.

> [!DIALOG]
> **Jorik:** "I had an opportunity to leave this city behind once." He looks at the iron in the fire. "I should've taken it."

> [!DIALOG]
> **Jorik:** "This forge once built the tools of sowing. Of merriment. Of harvest." A pause. "Shovels and plows. Harps and reins."

> [!NARRATION]
> He doesn't look away from the fire.

> [!DIALOG]
> **Jorik:** "All of it melted down now. Into weapons and shackles."

> [!NARRATION]
> The hammer rises. Falls once. He speaks quietly, almost to himself.

> [!DIALOG]
> **Jorik:** "With every swing I pray for her safety." A pause. He corrects himself. "No. Her abundance."

> [!NARRATION]
> He doesn't explain who. If the party asks — and they should — he'll tell them about Lisel. Not much. Just enough. She left when he wouldn't. She was right. He misses her.

> [!DIALOG]
> **Jorik:** "I thought I couldn't leave the forge behind. Couldn't learn a new trade at my age. Start over from nothing." He looks at his hands. "Then Dorogh came, and I had to learn a new way of living anyway." A beat. "Just not one I chose."
:::

:::details[🔑 The Maker Token | Jorik presses his mark into their hands before they go]
> [!NARRATION]
> He stops them at the door.

> [!NARRATION]
> He's got something in his hand — a small iron disc on a cord. Hammer-over-anvil, pressed clean into the metal. His mark.

> [!DIALOG]
> **Jorik:** "Take this." He holds it out. Not a gift — a tool. "It'll open doors in the district. People know what that mark means."

> [!NARRATION]
> He doesn't wait to see where they put it. He's already turning back to the forge.
:::

### At the Hollow — Amber Hollow

*These talking points occur after Jorik arrives at Amber Hollow — after the party brings word that Rjocht is open and Lisel is alive, and after Jorik sells the forge and walks west.*

:::details[🛒 The Arrival | Jorik comes west with a cart and a portable forge setup]
> [!NARRATION]
> He arrives without announcement. A cart, a horse that's seen better days, and a forge setup broken into pieces — bellows, a small anvil, tongs, hammers. Everything he couldn't leave behind, reduced to what fits.

> [!NARRATION]
> He asks where to set up. Someone points to the corner of the common yard. By evening there's smoke.

> [!DIALOG]
> **Jorik:** "Rocky ground." He says it looking at the fields. Not a complaint. An assessment. "I know what this needs."

> [!NARRATION]
> That evening, he meets his granddaughter.
:::

:::details[⚔️ After the Cave | The party returns from the ridge; Jorik listens to what they found]
> [!NARRATION]
> He's at the forge when you get back. He hears you coming and doesn't stop what he's doing — but he slows.

> [!NARRATION]
> When you tell him what you found up there, he puts the hammer down.

> [!DIALOG]
> **Jorik:** "I know that cold." He says it quietly. "Different cold than the ridge, but the same kind of wrong." He looks at his hands. "I've been feeling it since I got here."

*[Let them tell him what happened in the cave. He listens without interrupting.]*

> [!NARRATION]
> When they're done, he's quiet for a long moment. Then he picks the hammer back up.

> [!DIALOG]
> **Jorik:** "You did right." He strikes once. "That's all I've got."

> [!NARRATION]
> But something in the air is different. Or will be, soon. The cold off the ridge is already easing. The ground is waking up.
:::

## GM Notes

Give him weight. When Jorik speaks, slow down.

He gave the party a Healing Potion without asking for anything in return. He told them about Dorogh — not the military details, but the human cost. The people. What the city used to be.

He can serve as a safe return point inside Rjocht if the party needs a place to rest or regroup. Under Dorogh, a blacksmith is useful — weapons and chains must be maintained. Jorik has walked that line with integrity: making what he must, protecting who he can.

**Voice line:** "The fog's been here longer than I can remember. But fog doesn't last forever. Nothing does."
