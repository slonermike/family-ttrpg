Rothgar was a real soldier once. He served under Constance. When Dorogh came, he made a different choice than she did — and he has been paying for that choice in a way he would never name aloud.

He enforces Dorogh's will with precision. He does not enjoy it visibly. He does not look at the people he moves along or detains. He focuses on the rule, the violation, the action. If he looks at faces he might recognize something, and he cannot afford that.

He is not evil. He is a man who made a terrible compromise and has organized his entire life around not having to admit it.

## GM Notes

Rothgar is the most dangerous NPC in Rjocht because he is competent and not corrupt in the way Corrupted Guards are — he still has agency. He can make judgment calls.

**Redemption arc trigger:** A moment that forces him to choose between Dorogh's order and something undeniable. The party freeing Constance, or Constance looking at him through the window, could be that moment.

**If redeemed:** He opens a door. Turns away at the right moment. Does not ring the alarm when he should.

**If confronted with his past by the party:** He goes cold and hard first — "I did what I had to do." But something flickers.

**Voice:** Clipped, efficient, no wasted words. Does not threaten — states facts. "You are not permitted here. I suggest you leave."
