Long and low-slung, like a dog built for a place without light. No eyes — smooth skin where they'd be. Ears that rotate constantly, independently, sweeping the room. Nose always working.

It doesn't move until something else does. Then its head snaps toward the source. Then it moves.

**Motion Lock:** The Cavern Hound attacks the last player to make a significant movement. If no player has moved since its previous turn, it cannot target anyone — it pauses, head rotating slowly, ears sweeping. It knows something is there. It just can't find you.

**Standing perfectly still is a valid strategy.** So is sacrificing your movement to draw its attention while others act, using Creation to roll a stone in another direction, or coordinating so only one player moves per round. Reward players who think about this.

**It is very fast** once it has a target. Do not let players underestimate the moment they decide to move.

**It ignores sound from objects** (a thrown stone, a dropped item) after the first time. It learns. After one misdirection, it waits for breath and heat.
