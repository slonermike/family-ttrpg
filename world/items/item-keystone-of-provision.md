An iron keystone, plain and heavy. It was forged in the First Age for the city's general — the one who managed the stores, the grain, the winter supply. Whoever carried it held the city's survival in their hands. The role was called the Steward's Trust.

Dorogh carried it when he was appointed general. He opened the storehouses freely at first. Then carefully. Then only for those who earned it. Then only for those who obeyed. The keystone didn't change. The man who held it did.

Drachan didn't need to touch it directly. He just needed Dorogh to stop letting go.

**Using it:** The holder opens their hand, holds the keystone flat, and asks. The roll isn't skill — it's release. The harder it is to give away, the less it gives.
