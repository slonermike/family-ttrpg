:::details[👋 First Meeting | She thinks they stole his token — then she hears the truth]
> [!NARRATION]
> She crosses the fire without taking her eyes off what you're carrying. Stops just short of the light. The weapons behind you haven't moved.

> [!DIALOG]
> **Lisel:** "Where did you get that."

> [!NARRATION]
> Not a question. She already has a theory. She doesn't like it.

*[Let the party explain. Give it room. She listens without interrupting — which, you sense, takes effort.]*

> [!NARRATION]
> A long moment. Then, from behind you, the pressure disappears.

> [!DIALOG]
> **Lisel:** "He gave it to you." She looks at their faces, reading fast. "He's alive."

> [!NARRATION]
> She lets out a breath — and then she's laughing, loud and sudden, relief breaking like a dam. She spins toward the fire.

> [!DIALOG]
> **Lisel:** "Someone get them a plate! A big one!" She drops onto a bench like she owns the earth beneath it and looks back at you. "Sit DOWN. Tell me everything. Start with my father."
:::

She inherited Jorik's faithfulness and none of his stillness. She's loud when she's happy — which is most of the time — and terrifyingly quiet when she's not. She built this community the way she does everything: by deciding it was happening and making it so.

She left Rjocht two years before session 1 with five friends who saw what was coming before most people were willing to name it. She led them west. She found this valley. She said *why not here* and nobody had a good answer.

## Talking Points

:::details[🔨 My Father | Lisel hears the news; what she needs the party to carry back]
> [!DIALOG]
> **Lisel:** "I knew he wouldn't leave." She laughs, but it comes out a little unsteady. "I stood in that forge and I said 'Papa, please.' He just looked at me." She shakes her head. "Stubborn as iron, that man. Twice as hard."

> [!NARRATION]
> She reaches for the maker token without asking — picks it up, turns it over. She grew up with that mark on everything.

> [!DIALOG]
> **Lisel:** "He gave you this." Her voice shifts. Quieter. "He doesn't give those to people he doesn't trust."

> [!DIALOG]
> **Lisel:** "You tell him—" She stops. Pulls in a breath. "Tell him the valley's good. Tell him the harvest came in and we danced in the mud like idiots." She looks up. "He'll know what that means."
:::

:::details[🌅 First Light | She talks about sunrise here; how it leads back to her father]
> [!NARRATION]
> Catch her at the eastern ridge before sunrise and she won't say a word. She'll just sit. That's how you know this is the real one — she's never quiet for anything less.

> [!NARRATION]
> If you ask her about it afterward:

> [!DIALOG]
> **Lisel:** "He used to take me up to the top of the eastern wall. Before anyone was awake. We'd watch the sun come over the ridge and neither of us would say anything." She looks at the hillside. "I never understood why that was the part I missed most."

> [!DIALOG]
> **Lisel:** "I do it here too. Different ridge." She grins, a little sideways. "Same sun."

> [!NARRATION]
> A pause. Then, quieter:

> [!DIALOG]
> **Lisel:** "I think that's why I stayed, actually. The light in this valley in the morning." She shrugs, like it's obvious. "Reminded me of home."
:::

:::details[🌱 The Hollow | Lisel on how it started, what it costs, and why they stay]
> [!DIALOG]
> **Lisel:** "We didn't plan to stay. We stopped to rest and I looked around and said, 'Why not here? Water, shelter, nobody trying to hold it.'" She spreads her hands. "Mira had seeds. The rest of us had our hands."

> [!DIALOG]
> **Lisel:** "The ground's not kind." She says it plainly, no self-pity in it. "Rocky. Cold off the ridge. Doesn't rain when it should. We've broken more tools than I want to count." She looks out at the valley. "But it's ours. And this year we got something back from it."

> [!NARRATION]
> She looks at the table — the spread they've laid out, modest but real.

> [!DIALOG]
> **Lisel:** "My father made good tools. The kind that last." She shrugs, but it doesn't quite land casual. "I think about that sometimes."
:::

:::details[GM Notes]
Lisel is a grace note, not a quest giver. Don't load her with missions. Her role is warmth, rest, and the emotional payoff of Jorik's storyline.

**Physical presence:** She's rarely still. She gestures when she talks, sits cross-legged on surfaces that aren't chairs, laughs at things before they're finished being funny. She takes up space and doesn't apologize for it.

**The cold welcome:** Don't undersell it. These people read as wild and optimistic — that's real. They're also survivors who chose a defensible valley and know how to move quietly through it. The weapons appear fast. Then the warmth comes back just as fast. Both are completely Lisel.

**The land:** Cold from the western ridge (the cavern bleeding into the soil), warmth pressing in from the east (the fog breaking, the world waking). They're farming in the balance of two forces they don't fully understand. This year's harvest is modest but real — the best they've managed here. It feels like bounty because they earned every bit of it on rocky, grudging ground with worn-out tools.

**Sunrise:** She climbs the eastern ridge most mornings before anyone else is up. The party is welcome. She won't talk much. That's the point.

**Jorik's arrival:** When the party brings word that Rjocht is free and Jorik lives, Lisel sends word back through road contacts. Jorik gets the message, sells the forge, and walks west with a portable forge setup — tools shaped for hard ground. His arrival, combined with sealing the cave, is what tips the Hollow from surviving to flourishing.

**Thread — she has a daughter.** Jorik doesn't know he's a grandfather. Her daughter is young — born in the Hollow. If the party spends time here, they'll encounter her. Whether they carry that news back to Jorik is theirs to decide.
:::
