Bren knows the land around Amber Hollow better than anyone — the ridgelines, the water sources, where things grow and when. He's the reason the community eats well in the lean months. He solves problems with what's available, doesn't complain about what isn't.

He is very comfortable being the quieter one.

He doesn't talk about the ridge much. But he thinks about it.

## Talking Points

:::details[🌬️ The Ridge | He found the cavern foraging; the cold comes up from below, not from the air]
> [!NARRATION]
> He doesn't bring it up himself. But if the conversation turns toward the ridge, or the cold wind, or anything that prompts it — he sets down whatever he's working on.

> [!DIALOG]
> **Bren:** "There's a place up there I don't go anymore." He says it plainly, like a fact he's already made peace with. "Found it foraging. The berries grow thick on the backside of the ridge — always have. Good foraging up there, usually."

> [!DIALOG]
> **Bren:** "The rock goes black up near the top. Old burn, maybe, or just that kind of stone. There's a formation — looks almost like a doorway but it isn't. And under it, there's an opening in the ground. Not big."

> [!NARRATION]
> He's quiet for a moment.

> [!DIALOG]
> **Bren:** "Cold comes out of it. Not cold like wind. Cold like—" He picks his words carefully. "Like something on the other side of a door you don't want to open."

> [!DIALOG]
> **Bren:** "Symbols carved all around the edges. Old work, careful work — someone spent real time on those. They're..." He pauses. "Pretty, in a way. But standing there looking at them, I felt wrong. Like I was reading something I had no business reading."

> [!DIALOG]
> **Bren:** "The cold comes up from inside. From below." A beat. "I didn't go in. I came home. Haven't been back to that part of the ridge since."

> [!NARRATION]
> He picks up what he was working on again. Subject closed, as far as he's concerned.
:::

:::details[GM Notes]
Bren is the thread that leads to the next story beat. Don't push it — let the party find their way to it. If they ask about the ridge, about the cold wind, about anything unusual, he'll tell them. He's not hiding it. He just doesn't think about it more than he has to.

**The cavern:** Western ridge above Amber Hollow, under a blackened rock formation. Icy air rises from an opening in the ground — cold that originates below, not from the weather. Entrance ringed with carved symbols: intricate, unfamiliar, beautiful in a way that feels deeply wrong.

This is the threshold to the next tool of justice. The cavern is not the tool itself — it's the approach. What's inside is sealed, and the seal is old.

**The symbols:** Don't describe them too specifically. "Detailed" and "pretty" and "wrong" is enough. If someone tries to copy them, let them — but give it weight.

**Bren hasn't told Lisel everything.** He told her he found a cave and didn't go back. He left out the cold, the symbols, how long he stood there. He doesn't want her going up there. She would absolutely go up there.
:::
