    gm_notes: |-
      **Through or around** is the question that matters. People who came through the Stank — especially without a guide — earn immediate, grudging respect. People who went around are viewed as soft and probably untrustworthy.

      Coming from the eastern ridge (through the cavern) also earns curiosity, but not the same automatic credit. They'll want to know what was in the cave. If the party says "Blind Anger," the faces change. They know that name.

      **The ladder comes down** only if the party gives a satisfying answer. "Through it" is satisfying. A detailed honest answer about going around is acceptable if the party seems forthright. Bragging or performing bravado is not satisfying at all.

      **First interactions:** The people of Caedreth are not cold. They are reserved in the specific way of people who have learned that strangers usually want something and usually leave. They'll answer questions but won't volunteer information. They don't ask a lot of questions back — they watch instead.

      **Winning their trust:** Earn it through actions, not words. Fixing something, helping with a catch, spending the night without causing trouble, showing genuine curiosity about the swamp — any of these move the needle. A party that sits down, shuts up, and helps with work will be welcomed within an hour.

      **What they know:**
      - The Stank's causeways better than anyone — they'll share routes freely to people they trust
      - "The old anger" in the cave above the ridge — they stay away, always have
      - Something large in the deep center of the swamp — they call it the Lurker. They fish around it, not near it. It has never bothered them, and they intend to keep it that way.
      - They've heard of Rjocht. They don't talk about it much. It's east. East is trouble. They stay west.

      **What they need:** Left open for the GM. A good trust-earning quest: something disrupting their fishing lines in the western reaches of the swamp, a missing person from a routine crossing, a problem with one of the old causeways. Keep it grounded and practical — these are working people, not adventurers.

*Caedreth (KAY-dreth)* — a settlement built in the ancient trees at the northwest edge of the Swamp of Putrid Stank. The people here live above the swamp and make their living from it: fishing, trapping, harvesting the plants that grow in the margins, curing and smoking and selling what comes up from the water.

They are odd in the specific way of people who've lived in a small, high, isolated place for several generations. Warm to people they know. Reserved toward everyone else until proven otherwise. They have opinions about the right way to cross the Stank that they will share at length once they like you.

They do not leave Caedreth much. East is trouble. West is just more swamp. Up is home.
