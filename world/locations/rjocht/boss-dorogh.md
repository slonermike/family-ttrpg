One of Drachan's generals. Holds Rjocht in fear and cruelty. Kept Constance alive as a warning to the people. Never seen outside the fortress keep — his presence presses down like a physical weight.

**He is a corrupted man, not a demon.** He was once the city's general. He loved these people. He stood for justice. Drachan's whispers broke him: *"They betrayed you. They're weak. Only power matters. Take what you deserve."* The corruption is a shell. There is still a man inside.

Mercy is possible, but combat must bring him to the point of vulnerability first. It cannot be short-circuited by talking. At 0 HP, players are not required to deliver the killing blow — but Dorogh himself will ask for it. He has forgotten who he was. He cannot find his way back without help. The Blade's final blow is the merciful act: it breaks Drachan's hold and lets him remember.

## Throne Room

Magnificent but corrupted. High ceilings, stained glass (dark, cracked), marble pillars stained with shadow. Everything grand, twisted into oppressive. On the ancient throne: DOROGH. Massive — 8 feet tall. Blackened steel armor that drinks light. Horned helm, eyes glowing with cold hatred. Greatsword tall as a man.

**Opening:** *"Children playing at heroes. How many has the Order sent to die before me? Twenty? Fifty? I've lost count. Come then. Let me add you to the pile."*

### The Insight Moment (Triggers Phase 2)

When someone uses INSIGHT successfully:

*"You focus not on his armor or sword, but on HIM. You look past corruption, past hatred, past twisted power. And you SEE.*

*For just a moment, shadows flicker. Beneath armor, beneath rage, you see a man. A general who once defended this city. Who loved its people. Who stood for justice.*

*And you see what broke him: lies. Drachan's whispers. 'They betrayed you. They're weak. Only power matters. Take what you deserve.'*

*The corruption is a shell, and you've found the crack.*

*Dorogh SCREAMS — not in pain, but fury and... fear?*
*'DON'T LOOK AT ME LIKE THAT!'*

*His form shimmers. Shadows weaken. For the first time, he looks... vulnerable."*

## The Final Blow

When Dorogh reaches 0 HP:

*"Dorogh staggers, falls to one knee. Sword clatters. Shadows almost gone — you see him clearly. Just a man, broken and exhausted.*

*The opening in his breastplate glows bright. The shape of the Blade.*

*This is it. Only the Blade can finish this. Only the Blade can break Drachan's hold completely.*

*[Blade-bearer], the Blade is warm now. Not cold — warm. It knows what must be done.*

*Dorogh looks up, eyes clear for the first time. Human.*

*'Do it,' he whispers. 'Please. End this. I... I can't remember who I was anymore. I can't... please...'"*

**[Players can say final words — show mercy even in judgment. They are NOT required to strike immediately.]**

*"You raise the Blade. It gleams with clear, true light. You strike.*

*The Blade pierces the opening perfectly, through armor, through corruption, through lies, straight to his heart.*

*Dorogh gasps. And then... he smiles.*

*'Thank you,' he breathes. 'I remember now. I remember... the gardens. The people. Their laughter. I remember... who I was...'*

*The shadows shatter like glass. Light floods the throne room — real light, warm and golden. Dorogh's corrupted armor crumbles away. Beneath: just a tired old soldier, finally at peace.*

*He falls, but gently, like sleep after a long battle. The curse is broken."*

## GM Notes on Mercy

Dorogh is a corrupted human, not a functionally demonic entity. The Insight moment reveals this — what the player sees is the man Drachan destroyed. The mercy in this ending is not sparing him. It is releasing him. He dies remembering the gardens.

Players should feel the weight of the final blow as an act of compassion, not just victory. The Blade being warm — not cold — is the signal: righteous judgment and mercy are not opposites here.

Constance's words after: *"He was a good man once. Thank you for giving him peace."*
