Tam is young. Young enough that Rjocht under Dorogh is most of what he remembers. He became a guard because you did what you were told, and they needed guards, and his family needed to eat.

He is not cruel. He is scared. He has been scared long enough that scared is just how he is now.

His sister's kids haven't had meat in two weeks.

## Scene 4 — East Market Gate

Tam and Beren guard the East Market Gate, the traders' entrance. It is less watched than the main gates and the guards are not Corrupted — just conscripted men.

**Entry options:**
- **Heart or Wit 12+:** Talk through. Tam is the one who cracks — Beren holds out a bit longer. Finding a human thread (family, shared hunger, a kind word) is faster than authority or threats.
- **Merchant's ID:** Automatic pass. Tam waves them through without a word.
- **Disguise:** Tam is easier to fool. He desperately wants to believe things are fine.

## GM Notes

Tam is not the enemy. He is what happens to people when the people in charge stop caring about them. Play him with some dignity — he is not comic relief, he is a person making the worst of a bad situation.

If the party is kind to him, he remembers it. He cannot act on it in the moment, but he does not forget.

**Voice:** "I... you need to state your business. That's the rule. I'm sorry, I just — I need to hear it said. For the report. You understand." He does not actually want to cause trouble. He wants it to be over.
