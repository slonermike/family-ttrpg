Seraphine has a corner stall in the market with purple cloth and a bowl of water she looks into. Under normal circumstances she would be run out of a city like Rjocht. She has survived because Dorogh found her useful — she can sometimes tell him things, and she charges handsomely for it.

She has genuine Insight. That is the complicated part. The things she sees are sometimes real. She is not a fraud. She is a woman with a real gift who has decided to use it entirely for her own advancement.

## GM Notes

Seraphine will share information — for a price. Not necessarily coin. She wants something from whoever is asking: a secret, an advantage, a debt held over someone interesting.

She recognizes the Blade immediately if she sees it. Her reaction should be read by players as more significant than she lets on.

**She knows things she shouldn't:** Constance's location. Which guards can be talked to. That Dorogh doesn't sleep anymore.

**Do not make her a villain:** She is self-serving, not evil. She could be a complicated ally if the price is right and she believes the party might actually win.

**Voice:** Everything slightly more dramatic than necessary. Then a sudden flat, accurate sentence with no performance at all. Then back to the drama. *"The shadows have been restless for weeks. Something is coming through them. You, perhaps. That will be ten silver."*
