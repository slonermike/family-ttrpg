    gm_notes: |-
      **The smell is not a hazard.** It is a tone-setter. Lean into it — let the players react, make faces, complain. It's funny and it should be funny. The swamp is genuinely unpleasant but not dangerous if you know the way.

      **Finding the causeways:**
      - **Wit 10+** — spot the old stone markers just above waterline that mark the path
      - **Creation 10+** — test the ground ahead, find solid footing
      - **Insight 10+** — sense where the ground is firm vs. where it gives
      - **Failure** — sink knee-deep in mud. Not dangerous. Very funny. Can be pulled out with Might 8+ from a partner.

      **The smell has a use.** If the party needs something "clean, beautiful, and stinky" for the :npc[l1-carp-mermaid] — this swamp provides the stinky. A jar of swamp water, a mud-soaked flower still blooming. Let players make the connection.

      **What's in the water:**
      - Frogs. Many frogs. They watch you with enormous eyes.
      - Slow, sluggish fish that surface occasionally, look at you, submerge.
      - Something large moves through the deep center — visible only as a long ripple. It ignores you completely.

      **On the far side:** The trees begin again, and somewhere above them, if the light is right, you can see the underside of Netzer's platforms.

      **Time:** A prepared crossing takes about an hour. An unprepared one takes longer and involves more mud.

A wide, slow, genuinely foul-smelling expanse of wetland at the heart of Tzavesh. The water is dark and still and full of things that bubble up from the bottom for reasons the locals stopped investigating.

The people of Netzer cross it regularly. They know the causeways. They no longer smell it.
