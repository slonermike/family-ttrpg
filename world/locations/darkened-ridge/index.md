      - **Agility 10+** — climb up using whatever handholds present themselves

      - **Creation 10+** — improvise rope use, climbing harness, find a better route

      **If someone fails:** They slide back down. Not dangerous — just undignified. They can try again, or wait for someone else to create the opening.

      At the top of the ridge, near the mouth of a cave: a small creature, round and still, sitting with its enormous ears turned outward. It has velvet-dark fur, paws folded in its lap, and no eyes at all — just smooth fur where eyes would be.

      It heard you before you crested the ridge. It's been sitting here, facing your direction, since before you could see it.

      > [!DIALOG]
      > **Murn:** "Goob went in." He tilts his head toward the cave. "That was... I don't know how long ago. He hasn't come back. I can't go in. I tried." His ears flatten. "I can hear him. He's still there. He's not hurt. But he can't get back."

    gm_notes: |-
      Murn is a Wumble — a small cave creature that navigates entirely by sound, smell, and touch. He has no eyes. Don't describe him as sad or pitiful; he's frightened but composed. He's been sitting here for a long time and he's made peace with waiting. He just can't go any further.

      **Why can't he go in?** He tried. Something in the deep dark of the cavern makes a sound he can't process — a resonance that his enormous ears can't filter out. It's disorienting to the point of helplessness. Goob, whose ears are slightly different (they argue about this sometimes), can manage it, but Murn cannot. He doesn't describe this in detail — just says "I can't go in" with the matter-of-fact acceptance of something he's already tried many times.

      **He's not asking for a promise.** If the party says they'll try, he accepts that. If they say they can't guarantee it, he nods. He just needed to ask someone.

      **Heart check (optional, 8+):** A player who engages with Murn honestly — not just "yes we'll help" but actually listening — earns his trust. He tells them one useful thing about what's inside: *"There's something large in the deep part. It moves like it's angry. It doesn't move like it can see."*

      **He waits at the ridge top.** He won't come in. But if the party rescues Goob, he'll know the moment they emerge — he'll smell Goob before he hears him.

The western edge of the Reaches, where the treeline thins and the ground rises sharply into dark stone. The cold here is older than the fog over Rjocht — it lives in the rock.

The ridge separates Amber Hollow from the high caves beyond. Most people don't climb it. There's nothing up here they want to find.
