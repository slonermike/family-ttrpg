A weathered roadside tavern sitting at the junction of two trade roads, roughly halfway between the forest country and Rjocht's walls. The sign is faded but the lights are warm.

Late afternoon light comes through dusty windows and catches the smoke from the fire. Mismatched chairs, worn tables, a smell of old bread and woodsmoke. Not glamorous. Welcoming.

Under Dorogh's shadow the inn does steady business — people heading to Rjocht don't linger, but they stop here. Cheaper than the city. Safer in some ways.

## Why It Matters

Jasper was here in session 1. He provided:
- Intel on the East Market Gate as the safer entry into Rjocht (as opposed to the main gates, which are watched by corrupted soldiers)
- A positive social encounter before the grimness of the city

## Innkeeper

Unnamed. Practical, doesn't ask questions. Keeps the fire going.
