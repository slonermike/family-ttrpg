# Game Mechanics Reference

## Core Mechanic

- Player declares action and chooses an ability or item to use
- Roll 1d20 + 2d6 (pick the higher d6) + item bonus (if applicable)
- If you have 2 of the same ability: roll 1d20 + both d6 added together
- Compare total to threshold — must roll **above** threshold to succeed

## Combat Flow

1. Players attack: Roll d20 + ability dice vs. Defense Threshold
2. Enemies attack: GM rolls d20 vs. Attack Threshold
3. If enemy hits player: Player flips one ability card face-down (their choice)
4. If both abilities face-down: Player continues with items only
5. After battle: All abilities and armor flip face-up (restored)

## Damage System

**Players:**
- Take damage = flip 1 ability card face-down (can't use that ability)
- If both abilities are face-down: still in fight, use items only

**Enemies:**
- Track HP with checkboxes on enemy sheet

**Armor:**
- Absorbs 1 hit → flip face-down instead of flipping an ability card
- Restores at end of battle alongside all abilities
- Only cards explicitly marked **CONSUMABLE** are permanently removed after use

**Blade of Righteous Judgment:**
- Deals 2 damage to un-armored enemies

## Healing

- Healing items/abilities: Flip one ability face-up (player's choice which)
- Greater Healing Potion: Flip BOTH abilities face-up
- Can heal yourself or allies
- After battle ends: Everything restores automatically

## Resistances

- If enemy is resistant to an ability type, attack automatically fails
- No damage dealt, but ability is NOT flipped (no penalty)
- Player learns that approach won't work — try something else

## Loot Distribution

### Starting Loadouts
- **Blade-Bearer:** Receives ONLY the Blade of Righteous Judgment + Strange Bun
- **All Others:** Draw 3 ability cards (choose 2, discard 1) + 1 weapon + 1 item
- Players can trade weapons & items during setup (not abilities)

### During Adventure
- Small treasure: Draw 2 cards from Items deck, players share
- Medium treasure: Draw 3–4 cards, players pick in turn
- Major treasure: Draw 5+ cards, everyone gets something
- Enemy loot drops: Check enemy file for specific roll requirements

### Legendary Items
- Given at specific story moments (GM decides when)
- Boss victory: Each player draws 1 Legendary
- Major moral choice rewards: Sometimes 1 Legendary to party
- Earned through good choices, not random draws

## Difficulty Thresholds

Must roll **above** these numbers to succeed:

| Difficulty | Range | When to Use |
|------------|-------|-------------|
| Very Easy  | 6–8   | Basic tasks, favorable conditions |
| Easy       | 9–11  | Simple challenges, some skill needed |
| Moderate   | 12–14 | Standard difficulty, requires focus |
| Hard       | 15–17 | Challenging, needs good roll or right ability |
| Very Hard  | 18–20 | Expert level, tough even with advantages |
| Extreme    | 21–24 | Nearly impossible, boss-level challenges |
| Impossible | 25+   | Reserved for special circumstances |

### Adjusting On The Fly
- Party struggling: Lower thresholds by 2–3
- Party dominating: Raise thresholds by 2–3
- Kids don't need to know the numbers changed — keep it moving

## Common Rulings

**Can I use an ability for an unusual action?**
If it makes narrative sense or the player has a creative justification: yes. Reward creativity with lower thresholds. When in doubt, let them try.

**Can I use an item AND an ability?**
Yes — items give a bonus d6 to ability rolls. Example: Might to attack + Sword = +d6.

**What if I don't have the right ability?**
Roll d20 + item only (no ability bonus). Or use a different ability creatively with GM approval. Teamwork: a player with the right ability can assist.

**Can we work together on one action?**
Yes — one player leads with their ability, each assistant gives +1 to the roll (max +3). Must make narrative sense.

**Environmental / falling damage:**
- Minor: narrative consequence only
- Moderate: flip 1 ability face-down
- Severe: flip 2 abilities or use a major healing item

## GM Tips

**Keep It Moving** — Make a ruling and move on. "Yes, and..." beats "No." If kids are having fun, you're doing it right.

**Encourage Creativity** — Reward unusual solutions with lower thresholds. "That's clever!" goes a long way.

**Support the Blade-Bearer** — They carry no items or healing. Party must protect them. Give them spotlight moments: Blade warnings, the final blow.

**Balance Difficulty** — Watch for frustration or boredom. Adjust enemy HP on the fly. Enemies can flee if losing badly. Victory should feel earned but achievable.

**Celebrate Good Choices** — Kindness to NPCs pays off later. Mercy moments matter. Acknowledge when players embody the Fruits of the Spirit.
